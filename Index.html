SYSTEM ADHERENCE Renko Rule Compliance
01
Which of the 3 defined Renko setups does this trade match — exactly? Name it and describe the brick sequence that triggered entry.
If you cannot name the pattern and describe the exact brick count (e.g., "LL-HL: LL at 5 bricks down, HL held for 2 sessions, 2nd UP brick on HL break = entry"), you had a hunch, not a setup. A hunch on a ₹2L account produces the same outcome as 2022. This is the first and most important gate.
System
02
Was the Nifty 50 Renko in an uptrend at entry? Did the index correlation filter pass before I entered?
Taking a long LL-HL setup while Nifty Renko is in a downtrend removes the single most powerful edge in the system — index tailwind. This filter is not optional. A win in this scenario is luck, not process, and it trains the wrong behavior for when it eventually fails catastrophically.
System
03
What VSA signal confirmed entry? Write the exact ratio: today's volume ÷ 20-day average. Was it Demand Bar, No Supply, or high-volume breakout?
"Volume looked high" is not data. If the ratio is below 1.0 on an LL-HL breakout, no institutional participation confirmed the move. This number, tracked across 20+ trades, will statistically prove whether VSA filters actually improve your win rate — or whether you should adjust the confirmation threshold.
System
04
Was the stop loss placed exactly 2 bricks below the HL (or entry level) and entered as a hard SL-M order in Zerodha before I closed the screen?
A stop loss that exists in your memory and not in Zerodha's order book is not a stop loss — it is a wish. For a 9-to-5 professional who cannot monitor the market, this question is non-negotiable. Binary: yes or no. There is no "I'll place it tomorrow morning."
System
05
If this trade lost — looking back without result bias — was the LL-HL setup genuinely valid at entry? Or did I misread the brick count, the HL confirmation, or the VSA signal?
This separates a valid setup that lost (statistical noise — accept, don't adjust rules) from a misread signal (execution error — identify which check you skipped and fix it). Tracking across 30+ trades reveals the answer: if >60% show "setup was valid," the system edge needs refinement. If >60% show "I misread," the checklist execution needs reinforcement.
System
PSYCHOLOGICAL FRICTION 9-to-5 Stress Audit
06
Was I carrying emotional weight from work — deadline pressure, frustration, a difficult interaction — at the time I analyzed and placed this trade?
Stress from your 9-to-5 is the most under-documented driver of retail trading losses for working professionals. Cortisol impairs the prefrontal cortex — the exact neural circuit needed to say "the HL hasn't been confirmed yet, I'll wait one more session." Name the emotional state. It is data, not weakness. Trades placed in this state are statistically less reliable even with valid signals.
Self
07
Did I hesitate on a valid LL-HL signal because of memories of past losses? Or did I force an entry on a weak setup because I feared missing a move after watching bricks form without me?
Since 2022, every failed trade has left a memory trace that creates either paralysis (hesitation on valid setups) or overcompensation (FOMO entries on weak setups). These are opposite behavioral failures with the same financial outcome. Name which one showed up today — they have completely different corrective actions.
Self
08
Did I check my open Renko positions on my phone during work hours after placing the entry? If yes — how many times, and what did I do with what I saw?
Renko ATR/2 on Daily charts is specifically designed to eliminate the need for intraday monitoring. Every mid-day phone check is a symptom of trust deficit in the system — and typically leads to premature exits at the 1st pullback brick, which is statistically the most common exit point before a trade achieves its 4-brick target. The GTT and SL-M orders are placed. There is nothing actionable between 10:00 AM and 3:20 PM.
Self
09
Did I follow the written exit plan — SL hit = accept and move on, target hit = book, 2-brick reversal = exit — or did I improvise based on how I felt about the stock at that moment?
Your written exit plan was created by your calm, analytical self during the evening session. The in-trade decision is made by your stress-loaded, emotionally engaged self during market hours or a quick phone check. These are not the same decision-maker. Track which version is executing your trades. By Week 4 of journaling, the pattern will be unmistakable.

10
On a scale of 1–10: how well did I follow the Renko process this week — completely independent of P&L? What is ONE specific Renko rule I commit to protecting next week?
The process score decouples self-evaluation from random market outcomes. A trader who scores 9/10 on process but has a losing week due to statistical variance is on the correct path. If 60%+ show "FOMO" in Q7 or "checked phone" in Q8, the strategy is fine — the behavior is the problem.

Five Non-Negotiable Rules
01
Recalculate ATR/2 brick size every Monday

ATR changes week to week. A stale brick size generates wrong signals and wrong position sizes. Takes 2 minutes — no exceptions.

02
Never enter on the 1st UP brick — always wait for the 2nd

The 1st brick is a hypothesis. The 2nd brick is confirmation. This single rule eliminates the majority of LL-HL false starts and whipsaws.

03
SL-M order must be in Zerodha before 4:00 PM

No mental stops. No "I'll place it tomorrow morning." If the order is not in the system, the position is unprotected overnight — unacceptable for a 9-to-5 trader.

04
VIX above 20 = all position sizes halved

High volatility makes ATR/2 bricks unreliable and increases whipsaw risk. Half size = half loss if wrong. This is not optional on VIX-elevated days.

05
Maximum 3 open trades at any time

More than 3 means you cannot adequately review the Renko status of each during your 40-minute EOD window. 3 is both the cognitive limit and the capital management limit.
